# Maintane Avatar And Awareness Map

Use this map before building new pages, blog posts, emails, ads, or backlink assets. The goal is to match each visitor's current problem, awareness level, and trust requirement instead of writing generic "septic owner" content.

## Operating Principle

Maintane should win by being clear, useful, and specific. Most visitors do not wake up wanting a septic treatment powder. They arrive because something feels wrong, unsafe, confusing, expensive, or easy to forget.

For every new asset, define:

1. Avatar: who is this for?
2. Awareness: what do they already know?
3. Pain: what made them search?
4. Hook: what earns attention?
5. Proof: what makes the claim believable?
6. Next step: checklist, dosing guide, related page, or product.

## Awareness Ladder

### 1. Unaware

The visitor owns or may own a septic system but is not actively thinking about maintenance.

- Primary goal: create useful reminders and light education.
- Best assets: seasonal checklists, homeowner calendars, new-homeowner guides, social posts, Pinterest assets.
- CTA: free checklist or maintenance calendar.
- Avoid: aggressive product pitching.

### 2. Problem Aware

The visitor noticed a smell, slow drain, bubbling toilet, alarm, wet yard, or recent septic worry.

- Primary goal: help them identify what may be happening and when to call a pro.
- Best assets: symptom pages, triage blogs, FAQ sections, printable checklists.
- CTA: get the checklist, read the dosing guide, then shop if maintenance fit is clear.
- Avoid: implying Maintane fixes backups, clogs, failed drain fields, or full tanks.

### 3. Solution Aware

The visitor knows septic care exists and is comparing routines, product types, and household habits.

- Primary goal: explain why a simple monthly biology-first routine is better than panic buying.
- Best assets: natural treatment, chemical-free treatment, monthly treatment, septic-safe cleaning pages.
- CTA: dosing guide or first jar.
- Avoid: over-technical language and vague "natural" claims.

### 4. Product Aware

The visitor is comparing Maintane with alternatives or wants details before buying.

- Primary goal: make the product facts obvious and credible.
- Best assets: comparison pages, powder vs liquid pages, Rid-X alternative page, how-it-works page, review/proof sections.
- CTA: shop Maintane - $39.99.
- Avoid: hiding price, dose, limits, or product fit.

### 5. Most Aware

The visitor is ready to buy or has already interacted with Maintane.

- Primary goal: reduce friction and remind them how simple the routine is.
- Best assets: product CTAs, dosing guide, post-opt-in offer, retargeting, email follow-up.
- CTA: shop, get first jar, reorder, or subscribe when available.
- Avoid: sending ready buyers back into broad education loops.

## Content Allocation Rule

Use the Hormozi-style give/ask balance across the full content system:

- 70% give: education, triage, checklists, how-to pages, safety guidance, plain answers.
- 20% bridge: product-fit education, dosing guide, comparisons, how Maintane works.
- 10% ask: direct product CTA, post-opt-in offer, retargeting, most-aware buy pages.

This keeps the site useful enough to earn organic trust while still giving warm visitors a clear path to buy.

## Hook Formula For Maintane

Use this order when writing page intros, ads, emails, and social hooks:

1. Proof or plain fact.
2. Promise or useful outcome.
3. Plan or next step.

Examples:

- "One level scoop per toilet, once a month. A simpler septic routine for homes that want no harsh chemicals. Start with the dosing guide."
- "A septic smell can come from more than the tank. Check the pattern first, then decide whether it is maintenance or a pro issue."
- "$39.99 per jar. Kid and pet safe. Built for simple monthly septic maintenance."
- "Your septic system is biological. Maintane supports the bacteria your system already depends on with a monthly powder routine."
